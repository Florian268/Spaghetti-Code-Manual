What is Modularization?

Modularization is the practice of subdividing a system into smaller parts called modules. These interchangeable modules give a program configurability by being swappable to a client's needs. Subdividing creates a structured approach moving complexity on a modular level rather than system-wide. By grouping modules, there's increased safety by ensuring resource requests transfer through modules rather than having direct access. Lastly, smaller modules reduce maintenance.
